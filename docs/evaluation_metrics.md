# Evaluation Metrics

UHV should be evaluated with measurable indicators. The goal is to separate conceptual promise from validated engineering data.

## Thermal Metrics

* **Ambient temperature change:** air temperature before, during, and after operation at defined distances from the vehicle.
* **Road surface temperature change:** infrared or contact measurements on asphalt or concrete near the vehicle path.
* **Pedestrian comfort:** thermal comfort surveys or indices that include temperature, humidity, air velocity, and radiant heat.
* **City-scale deployment effect:** aggregated route-level or district-level effects from multiple vehicles, measured through controlled pilots rather than assumed from a single unit.

## Mist and Water Metrics

* **Mist evaporation rate:** fraction of sprayed water that evaporates before reaching the ground or nearby surfaces.
* **Water consumption:** liters per hour and liters per route kilometer.
* **Road wetting:** observed or sensor-measured liquid water on the road, sidewalks, vehicle body, or nearby objects.
* **Visibility impact:** optical or observational assessment of mist density and driver/pedestrian visibility.

## Energy Metrics

* **Generated auxiliary power:** electrical output from AER-Loop or parked natural-wind systems.
* **Auxiliary power reduction:** reduction in grid, battery, or alternator load for fans, sensors, controllers, or emergency systems.
* **Driving efficiency impact:** change in vehicle energy use per kilometer after adding retrofit hardware.
* **Drag increase:** aerodynamic penalty from external modules or airflow recovery devices.

## Operational Metrics

* **Maintenance cost:** labor, parts, cleaning, filters, water treatment, and downtime.
* **Reliability:** operating hours between failures.
* **Water tank sanitation:** cleanliness, biofilm control, and service interval.
* **Regulatory readiness:** permits, inspections, and compliance gaps for the target region.

## Reporting Format

Useful reports should include:

* location and date
* weather conditions
* vehicle type
* speed range
* water flow rate
* electrical consumption
* generated auxiliary power
* sensor placement
* safety observations
* raw or summarized data
* limitations and next test questions

No metric should be interpreted in isolation. A system that cools air but causes wet roads, high water use, or significant drag may not be practical without redesign.
