# Retrofit Implementation Plan

The retrofit path allows UHV concepts to be tested without building an entirely new vehicle. External Center-Mist Cooling units can be mounted as controlled modules, measured independently, and improved before deeper integration with airflow recovery systems.

## Target Vehicles

Potential retrofit targets include:

* buses
* delivery trucks
* taxis
* emergency vehicles
* airport vehicles
* port vehicles
* construction vehicles
* agricultural vehicles
* railway vehicles
* ships

These platforms often operate on repeated routes or in heat-exposed areas, which makes measurement easier and may support focused pilot programs.

## Retrofit Unit Concept

A practical external unit could include:

* ultrasonic mist generator
* fan or airflow duct
* center injection port
* water tank
* pump and filter
* humidity sensor
* ambient temperature sensor
* road-surface temperature sensor
* water-level sensor
* controller with safety shutoff
* waterproof electrical enclosure
* serviceable mounting frame

Early units should prioritize measurement and safety over maximum cooling output.

## Phased Implementation

### Phase 1: Tabletop Tests

Test mist generation, droplet size, evaporation rate, airflow mixing, water consumption, and electrical consumption under controlled indoor conditions.

### Phase 2: Bicycle and Cart Tests

Mount a small unit on bicycles, hand carts, or utility carts to study vibration, airflow, spray direction, and road-wetting risk at low speed.

### Phase 3: Bus and Delivery Vehicle Demonstration

Install a controlled external unit on buses or delivery vehicles. Measure temperature, humidity, water use, pedestrian exposure, visibility, and maintenance effort.

### Phase 4: Dry-Climate Field Test

Run field trials in dry climates where evaporation is more favorable. Compare routes, vehicle speeds, water flow settings, and time-of-day effects.

### Phase 5: Vehicle-Integrated AER-Loop

After cooling behavior is understood, evaluate whether auxiliary airflow recovery can support fans, sensors, controllers, or emergency lighting without causing unacceptable drag.

### Phase 6: Urban Transport Network Integration

If field data supports the concept, coordinate multiple vehicles across bus routes, logistics fleets, airport operations, port operations, or urban heat-stress zones. City-scale effects require careful measurement and should not be assumed from one vehicle test.

## Validation Principle

Each phase should separate conceptual assumptions from measured results. Reports should include failed tests, safety issues, water use, and operating limits, because those results are essential for responsible development.
