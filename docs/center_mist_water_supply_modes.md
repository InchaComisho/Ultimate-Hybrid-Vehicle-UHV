# Center-Mist Water Supply Modes

## Overview

Center-Mist Cooling requires a reliable water supply.  
However, the most suitable water source depends strongly on regional climate, rainfall, dust levels, maintenance capacity, and vehicle operation patterns.

A single universal water-supply method is not ideal.  
The UHV concept therefore treats mist water supply as a regional design variable.

---

## Mode 1: External Recovered-Water Supply

This mode uses externally recovered water sources, such as:

- rainwater collected from parking roofs, depots, buildings, or urban infrastructure
- air-conditioner drain water
- treated non-potable water
- depot-side filtered water supply

This mode may be suitable for regions with year-round rainfall, urban drainage systems, vehicle depots, parking structures, or building-integrated water recovery.

It is especially suitable when vehicles repeatedly return to the same depot, station, logistics hub, or parking facility.

---

## Mode 2: Direct Tank Refill

This mode uses direct refill into an onboard tank, for example:

- tap water
- bottled water
- filtered service water
- manually supplied depot water

This mode may be more realistic in arid regions such as parts of the Middle East, where rainfall is limited and dust or sand may make open external water recovery systems difficult to maintain.

Direct tank refill allows water quality to be controlled more easily, but it requires regular refilling and tank maintenance.

---

## Why Arid and Dusty Regions May Prefer Direct Tank Refill

In desert or dry-climate regions, dust, sand, mineral particles, and airborne debris may create high clogging risk.

Possible issues include:

- ultrasonic mist module clogging
- filter saturation
- mineral scaling
- pump damage
- biofilm growth in stored water
- reduced mist output
- short maintenance intervals

For this reason, Middle East dry-climate deployment may initially prefer sealed tanks, filtered refill water, replaceable cartridges, and short maintenance cycles rather than open rainwater-recovery systems.

---

## Why Rainy Regions May Prefer Dual Supply

In regions with frequent rainfall, two-system supply may be beneficial:

1. recovered rainwater / drain water supply
2. direct tank refill backup

This allows vehicles to use recovered water when available while retaining direct refill capability during dry periods, emergency use, or maintenance intervals.

---

## Engineering Requirements

Any water-supply method requires:

- filtration
- mineral management
- anti-clogging design
- tank cleaning
- pump protection
- sensor monitoring
- water-level detection
- maintenance alerts
- safe drainage
- hygiene control

## Caution

This document describes a conceptual water-supply architecture.  
It does not validate a specific ultrasonic mist module, filter design, water quality standard, or regional deployment method.

Real-world deployment requires engineering testing under dust, humidity, temperature, vibration, water-quality, and maintenance conditions.

The actual suitability of each supply mode depends on local infrastructure, regulatory requirements, water treatment capacity, vehicle operation patterns, and maintenance resources.

This is a conceptual model only. No specific performance, water quality, or reliability claim is made.
