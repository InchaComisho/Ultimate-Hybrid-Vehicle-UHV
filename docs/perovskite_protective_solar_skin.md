# Perovskite Protective Solar Skin

## Overview

Perovskite Protective Solar Skin is a conceptual UHV parking-energy subsystem. It proposes using protected, body-integrated perovskite solar modules to support auxiliary power while a vehicle is parked.

This is not a claim that perovskite solar cells are ready for all vehicle exterior use. Vehicle exterior use requires durability validation for sunlight, heat, moisture, vibration, impact, washing, dust, scratches, and long-term sealing. The intended role is auxiliary power support, not main traction power.

## Why Protection Is Required

Perovskite solar cells can be sensitive to moisture, oxygen, ultraviolet exposure, heat, mechanical stress, and surface damage. For vehicle exterior integration, the cells should not be directly exposed.

The concept therefore treats the solar skin as a protected module. It should include transparent hardcoat, UV protection, moisture barrier, encapsulation, insulation, and replaceable exterior protective layers. These layers may reduce output, but they are necessary candidates for durability and safety.

## Layered Protective Structure

```text
transparent hardcoat layer
↓
UV protection layer
↓
impact-resistant transparent film or panel
↓
moisture barrier layer
↓
encapsulation layer
↓
perovskite solar cell layer
↓
insulation layer
↓
vehicle body panel
```

This stack is representative, not a final manufacturing specification. Each layer would require optical, electrical, thermal, mechanical, and weathering validation.

## Candidate Vehicle Integration Zones

Candidate zones include:

* roof panel
* hood
* trunk or rear hatch
* upper side body panels
* sun-exposed exterior accessory panels
* replaceable parking-energy module panels

Integration must avoid interfering with crash safety, visibility, sensors, antennas, cameras, doors, service panels, charging ports, and emergency access.

## Relationship With Parking Mist Shield

The solar skin may support low-power standby functions related to the [Rainwater-Recovered Parking Mist Shield](rainwater_parking_mist_shield.md), such as sensors, water-quality monitoring, valves, control electronics, and limited mist standby. It should not be assumed to run high-power cooling continuously.

If mist or rainwater contacts the solar skin, the protective layers must be validated for water exposure, residue, cleaning, mineral deposits, UV aging, and electrical insulation.

## Auxiliary Battery Charging

The primary target is auxiliary battery maintenance. Possible uses include:

* maintaining a 12 V or low-voltage auxiliary battery
* offsetting standby sensor loads
* supporting communication beacons
* supporting low-power ventilation control
* supporting water-quality monitoring
* supporting parked security monitoring

Main traction battery charging is a separate safety topic. Any support to the main battery requires BMS integration, DC-DC control, reverse-current protection, insulation, fault detection, thermal monitoring, and safety certification.

## Material Candidates

Possible material concepts include:

* tempered glass
* acrylic
* polycarbonate
* PET film
* TPU film
* ETFE film
* vehicle paint protection film
* smartphone-like replaceable protective film concept

These are candidates for study, not final material selections. Vehicle use requires tests for optical transmission, yellowing, abrasion, stone impact, water sealing, thermal expansion, adhesive aging, and repairability.

## Maintenance and Replaceable Film Concept

A replaceable exterior protection layer may help keep the solar skin serviceable. The concept is similar to smartphone screen protectors or vehicle paint protection film: the outer layer can be replaced after scratching, staining, UV aging, or contamination.

Maintenance planning should include:

* inspection interval
* cleaning method
* replacement method
* water and detergent compatibility
* residue management
* electrical isolation checks
* module health monitoring

## Limitations and Validation Needs

Key limitations include:

* uncertain long-term perovskite durability on vehicle exteriors
* output loss from protective layers
* heat-related output reduction
* shading from buildings, trees, and other vehicles
* dust, dirt, and water spotting
* impact and scratch damage
* repair and replacement cost
* electrical safety certification needs

The system should be validated through weathering tests, thermal cycling, vibration tests, washing tests, insulation tests, and real parking trials.

## Summary

Perovskite Protective Solar Skin is a conceptual protected solar module for UHV parked auxiliary energy maintenance. It may support low-power standby systems, but it should not be treated as unlimited parking power or main battery charging without certified integration.
